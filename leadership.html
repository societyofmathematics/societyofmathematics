First we show that the stalks of the sheaf associated to $\mc O_{\red}$ are local rings; i.e., that $(X, \mc O_{\red})$ is a locally ringed space. Consider a point $p$. The stalk $\mc O_p$ is a local ring; quotienting by the nilpotent ideal yields another local ring $\mc O_p/\nil(\mc O_p)$. Clearly, on the level of stalks this is isomorphic as a presheaf to $\mc O_{\red}$; as such, it is isomorphic as a sheaf to the sheaf associated with $O_{\red}$ and $O_{\red}$ is a sheaf of local rings. Suppose $(X, \mc O)$ is covered by affine schemes $\spec A_i$. We seek to show that $(X, \mc O_{\red})$ is covered with affine schemes $\spec (A_i)_\red$. Note first that if $\mf p$ is a prime ideal of $A_i$, then $\mf p$ contains the nilpotent ideal $\nil(A_i)$; thus, quotienting by $\nil(A_i)$ gives a homeomorphism between $\spec A_i$ and $\spec (A_i)_\red$. Finally, on the level of stalks we have that the sheaf associated to $\mc O_\red$ is isomorphic to $(A_i)_{\mf p}/(\nil(A_i)_{\mf p})$, and so we have a scheme. The identity on $X$ is clearly a homeomorphism; the morphism given on the level of stalks by the first isomorphism theorem of rings yields a morphism of schemes.
